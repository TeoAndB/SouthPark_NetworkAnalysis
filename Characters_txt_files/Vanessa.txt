She appears to be depressed and is constantly abused by Vanity.\n\n==Appearance==\nVanessa wears a red vest with a white shirt underneath, a dark blue dress, and black shoes. She also wears red lipstick and has brownish-red hair and brownish-red eyebrows. Vanessa has visible wrinkles on her face and is always seen crying into a piece of tissue paper.\n\n<gallery widths=\"160\" captionalign=\"left\" caption=\"Images of Vanessa\">\nFreakStrike11.jpg|A photo of Vanessa along with [[Liane Cartman|Liane]] and [[Joline]]\nWatevailldowatiwant.png|Vanessa listening to Vanity and [[Eric Cartman|Cartman]] argue\n</gallery>\n\n==Personality==\nVanessa is an emotionally distraught, timid, and vulnerable woman, given her daughter's terrible behavior, along with her daughter's constant physical and emotional abuse towards her. She is also too weak to stand up to her daughter and discipline her, which is why she turns to Maury for desperate help. Vanessa often cries and is horrified to hear the shocking details of her daughter's immoral and illegal deeds, as well as Cartman's fabricated claims.\n\n==References==\n{{Reflist}}\n{{Minor Characters from Season Six}}\n[[zh:凡妮莎]]\n[[Category:Characters]]\n[[Category:Female characters]]\n[[Category:Gingers]]\n[[Category:Parents]]\n[[Category:One-off characters]]\n[[Category:Characters voiced by Eliza J.
