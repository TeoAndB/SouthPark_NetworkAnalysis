(TV episode 1998)|publisher=IMDb.com}}</ref>\n|wikipedia = [[Wikipedia:Charles Manson|Charles Manson]]\n|appearance = \"[[Merry Christmas Charlie Manson!]]\"\n}}\n'''Charles Milles Manson''' (née '''Maddox''') was an American criminal and cult leader, famous for his followers murder of actress Sharon Tate on his orders in 1969.\n\nHe appeared in the [[Season Two]] episode \"[[Merry Christmas Charlie Manson!]]\".\n\n==Background==\nManson breaks out of jail with [[Eric Cartman|Cartman]]'s [[Howard Cartman|Uncle Howard]], and they make their way to [[Mabel Cartman]]'s house to hide out. They arrive on Christmas Eve when Cartman and his family, as well as [[Kyle Broflovski|Kyle]], [[Stan Marsh|Stan]], and [[Kenny McCormick|Kenny]], are celebrating the holiday.\n\n==Appearance==\nManson, as he appeared in his debut episode, can be seen wearing an orange prison jumpsuit with a white shirt underneath. He has messy brown, graying hair and an untrimmed brown beard, also graying. He has a tattoo of a swastika between his eyes and has scars on his cheeks, which at the end of \"[[Merry Christmas Charlie Manson!]]\", he changes to a happy face tattoo.\n\n==Personality==\nInitially, Manson is filled with rage and psychotic anger.\n\nManson soon has a change of heart and regrets his mass murders after watching [[Mr. Hankey]] in ''The Grinchy-Poo Who Stole Christmas''. Realizing what he did was wrong, he changes his tattoo from a swastika to a smiley-face and tries to protect the boys from the gun-happy police.\n\nIn the end, Manson turns himself in to the police and returns to prison with Howard. He proceeds to write a series of positive help books, but his efforts are met with mockery. However, on Christmas, [[the boys]] and their families all come to visit Manson to celebrate.\n\n==Trivia==\n*Manson's tattoo of a smiley-face momentarily turns into a [[Visitors|Visitor]].\n*Manson's redemption is a parody of ''How the Grinch Stole Christmas''.\n*[[The Boys]] all come to address Manson as \"Uncle Charlie.\"\n*There are several times where it looks as if Manson intends to or has killed [[Kenny McCormick|Kenny]], only for Kenny to be revealed alive and happy.\n*Manson shows horror for Kenny's death while the boys do not show much more than their normal reactions.\n*His inmate number contains the number of the beast (666).\n\n==References==\n{{reflist}}\n{{Minor Characters from Season Two}}\n{{CriminalCelebrities}}\n\n[[es:Charlie Manson]]\n[[fr:Charles Manson]]\n[[zh:查尔斯·曼森]]\n[[Category:Characters]]\n[[Category:Male characters]]\n[[Category:Criminal celebrities]]\n[[Category:Scientologists]]\n[[Category:Elderly people]]\n[[Category:One-off characters]]\n[[Category:Characters voiced by Trey Parker]]\n[[Category:Deceased characters]]"
