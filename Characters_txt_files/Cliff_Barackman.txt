"{{South Park character\n|name = Cliff Barackman\n|image = Cliff Barackman.png\n|aliases = Elderly Man with a Long Goatee\n|gender = Male\n|race = Caucasian\n|hair = Gray\n|age = {{age|1970|11|28}}\n|dob = November 28, 1970\n|job = Cryptozoologist\n|appearance = \"[[Jewpacabra]]\"\n}}\n'''Cliff Barackman''' is a recurring [[City People|citizen]] of [[South Park (Location)|South Park]]. He is based upon \"Finding Bigfoot\"'s real person with this name,<ref>[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finding_Bigfoot \"Finding Bigfoot\"] on Wikipedia.</ref> and was first seen in \"[[Jewpacabra]]\" as part of the [[Bigfoot Researchers]] of town. In his debut he was voiced by [[Trey Parker]], in his next appearance he was briefly voiced in a grunt by [[Matt Stone]] in the end of \"[[A Song of Ass and Fire]]\".\n\n==Appearance==\nHe is a Caucasian male who has a widow's peak and a thin mustache, who sports a long goatee. While in his debut, he does not wear a hat. In his later appearances he is shown wearing a dark blue cap, alongside his usual attire consisted of a white shirt under a dark blue coat, light beige pleated trousers, and brown boots with light grey cover to the toes.\n\nHe bears much resemblance to [[James Cameron]]'s design.\n\n<gallery spacing=\"small\" caption=\"Images of Cliff Barackman\" widths=\"160\">\nJewpacabra00039.png|Cliff Barackman's debut in \"[[Jewpacabra]]\"\nSongofAssandFire00060.png|Cliff fighting a costumer in \"[[A Song of Ass and Fire]]\".\nTittiesandDragons00029.png|Seen standing in the right of the second row.\nSouth Park Citizens rooting for Mr. Mackey's song (''christmas Snow'').png|Seen drinking while rooting for [[Mr. Mackey]]'s song before Santa talks about the dangers of drinking and driving.\n</gallery>\n\n==Prominence==\n*\"[[Jewpacabra]]\" - Debut.\n*\"[[A Song of Ass and Fire]]\" - Can be seen fighting another customer in the [[South Park Mall]]'s parking lot.\n*\"[[Titties and Dragons]]\" - Seen waiting in the second line for [[Black Friday]] outside the South Park Mall while [[George R.
