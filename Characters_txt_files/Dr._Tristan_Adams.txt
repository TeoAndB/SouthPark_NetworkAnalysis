Observatory]]. He appears in the [[Season Two]] episode, \"[[Roger Ebert Should Lay off the Fatty Foods]]\" as the main antagonist.\n\n==Background==\nIn \"[[Roger Ebert Should Lay off the Fatty Foods]]\", Dr. Adams uses his \"star machine\" to hypnotize people into loving and volunteering for the planetarium.\n\nHe almost got away with it, but [[Eric Cartman]], pissed off, breaks the machine, resulting in Dr. Adams getting a full dose of the \"stars\". With no one to tell him what to do, his mind was erased. Due to a bone disease, he cannot pronounce the \"t\" in \"planetarium\".\n\n==Appearance==\nDr. Adams has many wrinkles in his face and is balding. He wears the standard uniform at the planetarium: A black shirt with a light blue v-neck shirt with a dark cloud and sun on it.\n\n==Trivia==\n*Dr. Tristan Adams is a parody of psychologist and director of the Tantalus Penal Colony in the ''Star Trek: The Original Series (1966-1969)'' season one episode Ten \"Dagger of the Mind\".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.memory-alpha.org/wiki/Dagger_of_the_Mind_(episode)|title=\"Dagger of the Mind\"|publisher=Memory Alpha, Star Trek Wiki}}</ref>\n*In the 1999 [[South Park (Video Game)|South Park video game]], entering the cheat \"PLANEARIUM\" unlocks a \"pen and ink mode\", which drastically alters the game's graphics.\n\n==References==\n{{reflist}}\n{{Minor Characters from Season Two}}\n[[zh:特里斯坦·亚当斯博士]]\n[[Category:Characters]]\n[[Category:Male characters]]\n[[Category:Adult characters]]\n[[Category:One-off characters]]\n[[Category:Characters voiced by Trey Parker]]\n[[Category:Deceased characters]]\n[[Category:Criminals]]"
