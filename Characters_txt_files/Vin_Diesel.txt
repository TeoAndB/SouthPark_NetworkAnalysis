"{{South Park character\n|name = Vin Diesel\n|image = VinDiesel.png\n|gender = Male\n|race = Caucasian\n|age = {{age|1967|7|18}}\n|dob = July 18, 1967\n|job = Actor, Director, Screenwriter, Producer\n|wikipedia = [[Wikipedia:Vin Diesel|Vin Diesel]]\n|appearance = \"[[Safe Space]]\"\n}}\n'''Vin Diesel''' is an actor who appears in the [[Season Nineteen]] episode, \"[[Safe Space]]\".\n\n==Background==\nVin Diesel is one of the many people [[Butters Stotch|Butters]] is helping filter out negative comments he receives, along with [[Eric Cartman|Cartman]]. He also sings the song \"[[In My Safe Space]]\", along with celebrities [[Demi Lovato]] and [[Steven Seagal]].\n\nHe also appears at [[Randy Marsh|Randy]]'s charity event for Shameless America. He tries, unsuccessfully, to protect Randy from being pressured into donating money at the [[Whole Foods Market]]. He leaves after the [[Whole Foods Cashier|cashier]] references Diesel's role in Fast and Furious, saying \"this little piggy was in Fast and The Furious\", which causes Diesel to break down in tears.\n\n==Appearance==\nVin's appearance may be taken from his roles in the ''Fast and Furious'' movie franchise. He is bald, wears a black sleeveless shirt with grey pants and brown shoes, and has a cross necklace. At the charity dinner, he is wearing a grey suit with a black undershirt. He is shown to seemingly have no noticeable flaws.\n\n==Personality==\nLike the other celebrities in this episode, Diesel seems to care deeply about how others think of him, so much so that Butters has to sift through all the negative comments and give him only the positive ones.\n\n{{Minor Characters from Season Nineteen}}\n{{MovieCelebrities}}\n[[es:Vin Diesel]]\n[[ja:ヴィン・ディーゼル]]\n[[zh:范·迪塞尔]]\n[[Category:Celebrities]]\n[[Category:One-off characters]]\n[[Category:Adult characters]]\n[[Category:Characters]]\n[[Category:Male characters]]\n[[Category:Movie celebrities]]"
